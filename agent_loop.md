# The Agent Loop

The agent loop is how an AI agent works step by step. It keeps repeating until the problem is solved. Think of it like a cycle: look, think, do, learn, and get better.

---

## The 5 Steps

### 1. Observe
The agent looks at the current situation and collects information.

### 2. Decide
The agent thinks about what to do based on what it observed.

### 3. Act
The agent takes an action.

### 4. Get Feedback
The agent checks what happened after the action. Did it work?

### 5. Improve
The agent learns from the feedback and adjusts its next action.

---

## Example: Ayesha's Learning Progress

### Step 1 — Observe
The agent looks at Ayesha's data:
- She has not logged in for **9 days**
- She completed **1 out of 4 assignments**

The agent notices she is falling behind and may need help.

---

### Step 2 — Decide
The agent thinks:
> "Ayesha is inactive and has low assignment completion. I should reach out to her."

Decision: Send a reminder email to encourage her to log in and complete her work.

---

### Step 3 — Act
The agent sends Ayesha a friendly reminder email:

> *"Hi Ayesha! We noticed you haven't logged in recently. You still have 3 assignments pending. We're here to help — come back and keep going!"*

---

### Step 4 — Get Feedback
After 2 days, the agent checks:
- Ayesha still has not logged in
- She did not open the email
- No assignments were completed

The action did not work.

---

### Step 5 — Improve
The agent learns from this:
> "A simple email was not enough. I need to try a stronger approach."

New decision: Escalate the situation by notifying Ayesha's teacher so they can personally follow up with her.

---

## The Loop Continues

```
Observe → Decide → Act → Get Feedback → Improve → Observe again...
```

The agent keeps going through this cycle, getting smarter with each round, until Ayesha is back on track.

---

## Key Takeaway

An agent does not give up after one try. It watches, acts, learns from what happened, and tries a better approach next time. That is the power of the agent loop.
